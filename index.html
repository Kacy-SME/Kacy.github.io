Research Highlights

Enhanced Detection of Martian Dust Devils

Developed a deep learning pipeline to detect low-opacity dust devils in over 23,000 Perseverance rover images, revealing 19 previously undocumented atmospheric events. This system integrates temporal differencing and domain-specific retraining to enable real-time environmental monitoring on Mars.

  • NASA Goddard
  • Computer Vision
  • Planetary Science

Read Paper · Code + Dataset

Fine-Tuned Motion Models for Insect Behavior

Adapted thin-plate spline motion transfer to track identity swaps in insect colonies without physical markers. Integrated SLEAP for pose estimation and visualized learned keypoints to interpret model mechanisms. Presented at CVPR 2024 CV4Animals Workshop.

  • Behavioral AI
  • Deep Learning
  • Biological Vision

Risks and Mitigations of AI-Powered Space Systems

Examines the ethical and technical risks of integrating AI into autonomous spacecraft. Proposes solutions using Particle Swarm Optimization to improve data replicability and reduce failure liability in scientific AI systems.

About

I am a PhD student in Computer Science at Arizona State University, working in the HEART AI Lab. My research explores the intersection of artificial intelligence, perception, and creative systems, with applications spanning planetary science, computer vision, and human-AI interaction.

I previously earned my Master of Science from ASU in December 2024, graduating as the Herberger Institute for Design and the Arts Outstanding Graduate. My master’s research focused on deep learning motion transfer and perceptual modeling for biological and extraterrestrial data.

At present, I am developing machine learning frameworks for scientific discovery — including real-time detection of Martian dust devils in rover imagery and methods that manipulate perception to reveal novel insights into biological systems.
